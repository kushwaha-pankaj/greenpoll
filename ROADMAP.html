Step 4: Single-Crop & Cross-Crop Transfer Learning

Understanding transfer learning gains with harmonization

Purpose

Understand transfer learning gains from (a) single-crop, (b) cross-crop joint training, with label harmonization.

Sub-steps

1. Harmonize Labels

Remap all crops to single "flower" class

  • Tomato: [class0, class1, class2] → [flower]
  • Strawberry: [flower, ripe, unripe] → [flower]
  • Apple: [flower] → [flower] (no change)
  • Kiwi: [flower] → [flower] (no change)
2. Single-Crop Baselines

Train YOLOv8n on each crop separately

  • Tomato only, Strawberry only, Apple only
  • Varying label budgets: [25, 50, 100, 200, 500]
  • 3 seeds each for reproducibility
3. Cross-Crop Joint Training

Train on combined [tomato + strawberry + apple]

  • Same label budgets and seeds
  • Evaluate on kiwi (held-out crop)

Outputs

  • Metrics comparing transfer vs single-crop performance
  • Understanding transfer learning benefits across crops

Timeline

Estimated: 4-6 weeks (significant training with label budgets × seeds)

Success Criteria

  • Harmonized labels created and validated
  • Single-crop baselines trained and evaluated
  • Cross-crop joint training compared
  • Analysis showing transfer learning benefits

Step 5: Full CrossPoll Pipeline

Active learning for annotation efficiency

Purpose

Active learning for efficient annotation — intelligently select which samples to label based on model uncertainty and diversity.

Components

  • Uncertainty Sampling: Query samples where model confidence is lowest
  • Diversity Sampling: Select diverse samples (feature-space distance)
  • Annotation Loop: Iteratively label, retrain, measure improvement
  • Comparison: CrossPoll vs random vs uncertainty-only vs diversity-only

Outputs

  • Annotation efficiency curves (mAP vs labeled samples)
  • Speedup factor vs random labeling

Timeline

Estimated: 2-3 weeks (implementation + evaluation)

Success Criteria

  • Active learning loop implemented
  • Annotation efficiency curves generated
  • CrossPoll vs baselines compared
  • Paper-ready results and visualizations

❓ Questions & Clarifications

Q: Why not train on all 4 crops together in Step 4?

A: Kiwi is held-out for unbiased evaluation. Training exclusively on [tomato, strawberry, apple] isolates the cross-crop transfer effect.

Q: What if COCO baseline is already very high (>80 mAP)?

A: Still valuable — shows transfer learning ceiling. Active learning might have limited room to help, but understanding this bound is crucial for the paper.

Q: When do we finalize the research paper?

A: After Steps 3-5 complete, with results and plots integrated into LaTeX manuscript in paper/manuscript/paper.tex
